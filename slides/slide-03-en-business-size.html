Canada's Employer Economy Is Built on Very Small Firms

Over half of employer businesses have just 1–4 employees.

Employer business size breakdown – Dec 2025
1–4 employees 56.5%
5–19 employees 30.3%
20–49 employees 8.3%
50–499 employees 4.6%
500+ employees 0.3%
Key Insight
Fewer than 5% of employer businesses have 50 or more employees.
Of 1.37 million employer businesses, only ~67,000 cross the 50-employee threshold — making large employers statistically rare in Canada's economy.
What This Means
Canada's employer base is highly exposed to economic shocks because most firms are extremely small.
Small firms carry less capital, fewer redundancies, and a narrower ability to absorb disruption — making the national employment base sensitive to sector-specific downturns.
