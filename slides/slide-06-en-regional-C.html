Canada's Business Landscape Is Increasingly Solo-Driven

Self-employed businesses now outnumber employer firms by nearly 3 to 1 in some provinces. The shift toward solo operators is reshaping what "business growth" means across the country.

Solo Operator Nation
Ontario (2.93×) leads the country — nearly 3 solo firms for every employer business.
Nunavut is the only jurisdiction where employers outnumber the self-employed (0.87×). Atlantic provinces cluster well below the national average of 2.67×.
Structural Shift
Most provinces now have more than 2 self-employed businesses per employer firm.
10 of 13 provinces and territories report a self-employment ratio above 1.3×, signalling a broad structural shift — not a regional anomaly. Canada's business base is becoming predominantly solo-driven.
